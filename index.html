PCC CS Club

Welcome.

Pasadena City College's Computer Science club hopes to elevate the members of our community by hosting talk's, workshops, and an active space for members to work on projects together.

In the past, we have hosted a wide variety of educational workshops addressing areas such as Machine Learning, Cyber Security, and Python and hope to continue this trend by culminating our fall semester in a hackathon!

We welcome any curious minds to participate in our program no matter what experince level.

Kody Kinzie
Public Relations

Kody is a security researcher (ethical hacker) and editor for Null Byte, a website that teaches over 1 million monthly readers about ethical hacking and computer science through engaging tutorials. He has worked leadership roles at logistics, fashion, and restaurant delivery tech startups developing mobile applications in Southern California. He is active in LA’s hackerspace and infosec community, managing pop-up hacker meetups around Southern

California.

Kody’s goal on the CS board is to bring together those who love technology with better resources, identify and support student leaders, and organize outreach to the community to ensure CS club is a resource for all members, regardless of coding background or experience.

Kody’s CS knowledge is centered around offensive hacking techniques, low-cost computing platforms like the Raspberry Pi, and red team tactics. A red team is a team that is paid to use the same techniques hackers use to attack the security of a business employing them, in order to fix any issues a real attacker might later exploit. Kody’s red team experience includes Wi-Fi attacks, NFC card cloning, social engineering, software defined radio, OSINT, and working with offensive linux distributions.
